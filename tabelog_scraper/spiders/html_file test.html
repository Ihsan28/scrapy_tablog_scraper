[Famous 100% Buckwheat Soba Restaurant at Sanze Pass] Iroha Gozen at Saigoku Goryo Furabo

Located in an area known as the Soba Road at Sanze Pass, Saigoku Goryo Furabo is a favorite of mine, having visited before and found it delicious. Founded in 1999, this long-established restaurant has been around for over 25 years and was featured in the Michelin Guide in 2019. As you descend towards Saga from the Sanze Tunnel toll booth, turn right at the sign, cross the bridge, and you will find it ahead. There is a spacious parking lot, and if you follow the small path next to the sign, you’ll discover a lovely garden. The building itself is a charming wooden structure reminiscent of a traditional Japanese house. Upon entering, you are greeted by a waiting area that spreads out to the left and right. The left side leads to the restroom, while the right side features glass windows where you can see the soba flour milling machines and noodle-making area. At the entrance, there is a notebook where you write your name, and when called, you can remove your shoes and step inside. To the left and right of the entrance are tatami rooms and sunken kotatsu seating, along with semi-private rooms. In front, there are table seats, and further back by the windows, there are counter seats. The high ceiling and spacious layout create a feeling of openness. After being seated at a table, I was quickly served soba tea, cold water, and a hand towel. The homemade soba tea was incredibly fragrant and delicious. While enjoying the tea, I looked over the menu. This restaurant specializes in 100% buckwheat soba, made with freshly peeled, ground, and cut soba. Their main offerings include shrimp tempura soba, assorted tempura gozen, cold soba with dipping sauce, grated soba, duck nanban, tempura rice bowl, and assorted platters. I ordered the Iroha Gozen, and while waiting, I admired the view from the window. Interestingly, they use locally sourced wasabi from Shizuoka, known for its great aroma. Soon, I was served a delightful tofu made from kudzu and soy milk, along with homemade miso and cucumber. Next came two types of salt, homemade soba dipping sauce, wasabi, and green onions, followed by the assorted tempura and the complete Iroha Gozen. I started by pouring the soba dipping sauce and prepared the tofu, which I had loved during my last visit. It was chewy and sweet, and the simple combination with soy sauce was incredibly delicious. The homemade miso was also delightful, pairing perfectly with the cucumber. Now for the soba. The 100% buckwheat soba was surprisingly smooth, with a firm texture and rich flavor that made it hard to believe it was 100% buckwheat. Even just eating it with plum salt, matcha salt, or wasabi was delicious. And enjoying it with the sharp yet easy-to-eat soba dipping sauce was absolutely fantastic! The tempura included shrimp, eggplant, okra, pumpkin, carrot, and shimeji mushrooms. While it was delicious with the soba dipping sauce, it was also great with salt. The tempura was incredibly crispy, especially the shrimp tempura, which was outstanding! *Please note that in the video, shimeji mushrooms were mistakenly labeled as maitake. I apologize for the error. After enjoying the tempura, dipping the soba into the flavorful broth enriched by the oil from the tempura was simply delightful. I finished everything deliciously and then enjoyed the soba-yu (hot water used to rinse the soba) to relax. The Iroha Gozen allows you to choose one of three types of soba dumplings, and since there were three of us, we shared kinako, soba dipping sauce, and zenzai. The soba dumplings were so fluffy yet chewy, and surprisingly light, making them delicious. The kinako was tasty, and I enjoyed the soba dipping sauce with wasabi, as well as the zenzai, but personally, I voted for the zenzai since I have a sweet tooth. Additionally, the side dish of karawabi, a sweetened wasabi stem cooked in soy sauce and mirin, paired excellently with the zenzai. I enjoyed it all with tea to conclude my meal. The owner has traveled across the country tasting soba and continues to refine his craft, and you can truly feel his passion and dedication to soba at this restaurant. If you have the chance, I highly recommend giving it a try!

A cozy restaurant specializing in 100% buckwheat soba

This is a story from late November when I sold an old house near Sanze Pass. On my way back from the handover, the seller kindly treated me to a meal at Furabo, a place in Fuji Town, Saga City, that I had been wanting to try. It was my first visit, and despite the unfortunate rainy weather, the restaurant was full. Luckily, I was able to sit at a table on the left side just as someone was leaving. The seating was a sunken kotatsu style, making it comfortable for me, even with my mobility issues. The interior felt like a traditional old house with high ceilings, giving it a spacious and open feel. From what I saw, there were tables and low tables, but no counter seats. While enjoying a fragrant hot tea, which I think was soba tea, I looked over the menu. The seller and his wife, along with myself, all ordered the Ebi Tempura Soba. Soon after, we were served some tofu and morokyu (a cucumber dish). The tofu was chewy and had a delightful flavor, likely enhanced by the inclusion of kuzu starch, as I could taste hints of sesame or soba. It was absolutely delicious! The morokyu was tasty, too. Next came the condiments: green onions, wasabi, dipping sauce, green tea salt for the tempura, and regular salt, followed shortly by the soba and tempura! The 100% buckwheat soba had a nice chewiness and rich flavor, making it extremely delicious. It seems they even mill their own flour, resulting in wonderful homemade noodles. I enjoyed it with salt instead of dipping sauce, and it was great that way too! The tempura included two pieces of ebi, along with eggplant, green beans, pumpkin, carrots, and shimeji mushrooms, all delicious. Finally, I ordered the soba-yu (hot water used to rinse the soba) to drink, thinking it was the end of the meal, but then they surprised us with kudzu mochi and more tea, which was also delightful. With the thoughtful design of the restaurant and dedication to the quality of their soba, it’s no wonder this place is thriving. I truly enjoyed my meal. Thank you very much!
